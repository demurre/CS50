Basketball

Basketball is a popular team sport played by two teams of five players each. The objective of the game is to score points by shooting a ball through the opponent's hoop. The team with the most points at the end of the game wins.

Basic Basketball rules:

  1. Teams: A basketball game is played between two teams, each consisting of five players on the court.
  2. Objective: The objective of the game is to score points by shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop and prevent the opposing team from scoring.
  3. The Court: The game is played on a rectangular court with a hoop at each end. The standard court dimensions are 94 feet in length and 50 feet in width.
  4. Game Duration: A basketball game is typically divided into four quarters, each lasting 12 minutes in professional games. In lower levels or youth games, the duration may be shorter.
  5. Starting the Game: The game begins with a jump ball at the center of the court. The referee tosses the ball up, and one player from each team tries to gain possession.
  6. Dribbling: A player must bounce the ball on the floor using one hand while moving. If a player stops dribbling or uses both hands, it's called a double dribble, which is a violation.
  7. Shooting: Players score points by shooting the ball into the opponent's hoop. A made shot inside the three-point line is worth two points, and a shot from beyond the three-point line is worth three points.
  8. Free Throws: When a player is fouled during the act of shooting or when a team commits a certain number of fouls in a quarter, the fouled player gets to take free throws. Each successful free throw is worth one point.
  9. Fouls: A foul occurs when a player makes illegal contact with an opponent. Common fouls include pushing, holding, and tripping. If a player commits too many fouls, they may be disqualified from the game.
  10. Violations: Violations are rule infractions that do not involve contact with an opponent. Examples of violations include traveling (taking more than two steps without dribbling) and stepping out of bounds.
  11. Timeouts: Each team is allowed a certain number of timeouts during a game to strategize and rest. In professional games, teams usually have multiple timeouts per half.
  12. Overtime: If the game is tied at the end of regulation time, additional time called overtime is played. The length of overtime varies depending on the level of play.
